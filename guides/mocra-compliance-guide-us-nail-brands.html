MoCRA Compliance Guide for US Nail Brands

Navigating the New Era of FDA Oversight for Professional Gel Polish.

Executive Summary

The Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act (MoCRA) represents the most significant change to U.S. cosmetics law since 1938. For American nail brands and importers, compliance is no longer optional—it is a prerequisite for market access. By 2026, mandatory Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) and stringent labeling requirements will redefine the competitive landscape. This guide provides a strategic roadmap for B2B buyers to ensure their supply chain is "MoCRA-Ready."

1. The Four Pillars of MoCRA Compliance

Requirement Deadline/Status Action for Brands NEW NAIL Support
Facility Registration Mandatory Ensure your manufacturer has an FDA Establishment Identifier (FEI). Valid FEI & ISO 22716:2007 (GMP) Certification.
Product Listing Mandatory Submit a list of each cosmetic product to the FDA, including ingredients. Full Structured Product Labeling (SPL) data support.
Safety Substantiation Ongoing Maintain records proving that products are safe for intended use. Professional Toxicological Risk Assessment (TRA) reports.
Adverse Event Reporting Immediate Establish a system to report serious adverse events to the FDA within 15 days. Traceable batch records for rapid root-cause analysis.

2. Labeling Transparency (2026 Readiness)

MoCRA mandates new labeling standards that impact both professional and consumer packaging:

  • Contact Information: Labels must include a domestic address, phone number, or electronic contact for adverse event reporting.
  • Professional Use Only: Products intended for use by licensed professionals must be clearly labeled as such.
  • Fragrance Allergens: Disclosure of fragrance allergens (to be defined by FDA) will be mandatory.
  • Fragrance-Free vs. Unscented: Strict adherence to ingredient claims to avoid "misbranding" charges.

3. Why Paperwork is the New "Quality Standard"

In the MoCRA era, a "good product" is defined by its documentation. NEW NAIL provides a "Compliance Packet" for every US client:

  1. SDS (Safety Data Sheets): Updated GHS-compliant documents.
  2. COA (Certificate of Analysis): Batch-specific testing results.
  3. 18-Free & HEMA-Free Certifications: Validating non-toxic claims for the premium market.
  4. Ingredient Transparency: Direct SPL-ready ingredient lists for FDA portal submission.

Professional FAQ for US Buyers

Q: What are the MoCRA requirements for nail polish specifically?

Answer: Beyond facility registration and product listing, nail brands must focus on safety substantiation. This includes ensuring pigments and photoinitiators (like TPO alternatives) meet FDA safety thresholds.

Q: Does MoCRA apply to small salon brands?

Answer: Yes. While some "small businesses" have exemptions for GMP, they are not exempt from product listing, safety substantiation, or adverse event reporting.
